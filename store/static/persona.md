## The Person

**Name (internal label):** *Toru*. 30, male, lives in Tokyo (港区 / 渋谷区).

### Work
- Senior IC at a venture-backed AI/crypto startup OR designer at a Tokyo studio
- Reads HN, follows ~200 carefully chosen accounts on X
- Salary ¥10–18M / year, no kids
- Owns a Vitra chair, a Linus bike, a Leica Q3

### Wardrobe (existing — buy these brands)
- Visvim, Y-3, Engineered Garments, Comme des Garçons Homme Plus
- Some Yohji archive
- Whites & blacks dominate, splash of natural cream
- 1 designed-T per week. Pays ¥10k–25k for a single shirt without flinching.

### Reads
- Hodinkee, Cool Hunting, Highsnobiety (skeptically), Stratechery
- Some Substacks: Not Boring, The Generalist, A Letter a Day
- Japanese: Wirefarm, スケジュール, casa BRUTUS Web

### Mental model that makes him buy MU
1. **Authenticity through process, not story.** He's read 30 founder stories and they all sound the same. MU's authenticity = "the temperature decided what got printed today". Numbers, not narrative.
2. **Anti-seasonal.** He's tired of "FW25" branding. MU's "no seasons" speaks directly to his fatigue.
3. **Quiet luxury.** He pays for products that don't shout. MU's logo (━◯━) is invisible at 1m. Perfect.
4. **Permanence + finite.** A drop sold out, never reprinted. He owns 1 of 108. Same logic that makes him pay ¥800k for a vintage Rolex.
5. **Tech tribal signal.** Other people in his circle will see MU and know what it is. Like wearing a Marc Andreessen / a16z tee but the opposite — it signals "I know but I don't need to brag".

### Why he doesn't buy yet
- Doesn't know MU exists (we have <100 X followers)
- No friend has worn one in front of him yet (we have 7 buyers, 0 visible)
- Site is "interesting but no specific shirt I want" — no hero piece to anchor desire
- Price feels right but he's never seen a real photo on a real person

### What converts him in 1 sentence
> "見たことのある形だけど、誰も持ってない。来週の今日には、もう買えない。"

(A familiar form but no one owns one. By next week today, you can't buy it.)

## Anti-targets

These people are NOT the target — say no to them:
- General fast-fashion buyers (Uniqlo / GU)
- People who buy primarily on price
- People who want logos / "branded" feeling
- Resellers / flippers (the 1-of-N constraint already excludes them, but no actively-courting)
- Non-Japan / non-tech-savvy crypto holders (they don't care about Constitution)
