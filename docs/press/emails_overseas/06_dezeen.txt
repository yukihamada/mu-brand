Concept in one paragraph:

MU is an apparel brand whose design and production decisions are
delegated to a system. An AI (Google Gemini 3 Pro Image) generates a new
T-shirt design every hour. The day's production run equals the
temperature, in Celsius, of the small Hokkaido town of Teshikaga — a
weather oracle, replacing trend forecasting with a natural-world signal.
Garments are manufactured on-demand (Printful), shipped with a Solana
cNFT certificate, and priced on a bonding curve: the first buyer gets
the cheapest piece, each subsequent buyer pays more. The design pipeline,
pricing engine, and order flow are all MIT-licensed open-source on GitHub
(https://github.com/yukihamada/mu-brand), so the claim of "no human in
the loop" is verifiable by reading the code, not by trusting the brand.

Why I think this is Dezeen-relevant:

1. **Generative design as primary creator** — not as a tool used by a
   designer, but as the operator. The cron job is the studio.
2. **Climate-as-design-input** — weather literally shapes the supply
   curve. A small-town microclimate signal becomes a global production
   constraint.
3. **Open-source architecture for autonomous brands** — replace the
   oracle, the prompt, the cadence, and you have a different brand.
   We've already shipped a Tokyo-data variant (MU CITY) to prove it
   forks cleanly.
